Benefits of practising yoga.

Practicing yoga offers a wide range of benefits for both the body and the mind. Here are seven key benefits supported by scientific research

‌

Mood Enhancement:

Written by Kate Silcox,YJ Editors Jan 15, 2014

Regular yoga practice may lead to increased mental and physical energy, a boost in alertness and enthusiasm, and fewer negative feelings, contributing to brighter moods.

Heart Health:

Written by Kate Silcox,YJ Editors Jan 15, 2014

The practice of yoga can benefit heart health, and this is connected to the "Asana" (posture) limb. Asanas such as "Sethu Bandhasana" (Bridge Pose) can help improve blood circulation and reduce stress, contributing to heart health. This is associated with the "Throat Chakra" (Vishuddha), which is located at the throat and is linked to communication and self-expression

Back Pain Relief:

Written by Allison Torres Burtka Jan 22, 2021

Research has found that yoga is a good alternative to physical therapy in easing back pain, and it comes without the side effects of medication

Stem Cells:

Written by Nitya Shree, Ramesh R. Bhonde Jul–Sep 2016

Ation of yoga on stem cell trafficking could contribute to the body's natural repair and regeneration processes, enhancing the overall health and well-being of an individual . Therefore, yoga's influence on stem cell trafficking and potential tissue repair further underscores its holistic impact on the body's physiological processes.

Better Sleep:

Written by Allison Torres Burtka Jan 22, 2021

Yoga provides an effective solution to sleep problems without negative side effects. It can help individuals fall asleep more easily, wake up less frequently, and feel more refreshed in the morning.

Improved Strength, Balance, and Flexibility:

Written by Allison Torres Burtka Jan 22, 2021

Yoga movements help build muscle strength by using the body's weight as a form of resistance. Holding the poses and moving through them while doing yoga also helps build endurance
